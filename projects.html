Representative Work

A few examples of how we turn messy, real-world requirements into working software, integrations, and automations that teams actually use.

Physical Security Operations Portal

Customer: Enterprise campus environment
Focus: Access control, video, and event visibility

Designed and implemented a browser-based portal that unified live access control data, door status, muster reports, and camera links into a single, operator-friendly view.

  • Integrated with existing access control and VMS platforms.
  • Muster reporting and “who is on site” visibility in near real-time.
  • Floorplan-based navigation so operators see the problem in context.

Result: Faster response, fewer blind spots, and less time spent jumping between vendor consoles.

Portfolio Operations & Maintenance Tracking

Customer: Multi-property real estate portfolio
Focus: Workflows, status tracking, and reporting

Built a lightweight internal system to track renovations, work orders, inspections, and capital projects across multiple properties.

  • Single source of truth for scope, cost, and schedule by property.
  • Automated status dashboards for owners and project managers.
  • Exportable data for accounting and tax support.

Result: Owners get a clear view of what’s happening where, and field work stays aligned with budget and timeline.

Secure Admin Portal & Identity Integration

Customer: SaaS / security technology platform
Focus: Admin UX, access control, and integrations

Created a secure administrative portal backed by Cloudflare Zero Trust and identity provider integrations, enabling a clean, modern management experience without exposing internal tools directly to the internet.

  • Protected admin surface behind SSO and Zero Trust policies.
  • Clean, task-oriented UI for common operational workflows.
  • Structured API layer designed for future automation and reporting.

Result: A professional-grade admin experience with strong security controls and room to grow.

What These Projects Have in Common

  • They sit in the middle of existing tools and constraints.
  • They put operators and stakeholders first, not just technology.
  • They are built to be extended, documented, and supported.
  • They produce artifacts decision-makers can see and react to.
